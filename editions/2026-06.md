## Findings this month

**Zep Community Edition is dead — and most of the internet hasn't noticed.**
The `getzep/zep` repo (4.6k stars) is now "Examples, Integrations & More" only.
The open-source piece of the Zep family is **Graphiti**, the temporal knowledge
graph engine. If a guide tells you to self-host Zep CE, it's stale.

**claude-mem is the adoption story of the year.** 81k stars for a session-capture →
AI-compression → context-reinjection layer for coding agents (Claude Code, Codex,
Copilot, and others). Whether retrieval quality justifies the hype is exactly what
the benchmark phase will test.

**The "weird idea" entrants are real projects.** Memvid (memory encoded via video
codecs, 15.6k stars) and Hindsight (separate fact/experience/opinion networks, by
Vectorize, MIT) are both alive and shipping. Hindsight claims SOTA on long-horizon
benchmarks — noting for the record that Vectorize also publishes tool comparisons,
so those claims get the same independent verification as everyone else's.

**The category is consolidating into four shapes.** Fact extractors (Mem0, Memori),
graph builders (Graphiti, Cognee, mcp-knowledge-graph), profile/user-modelers
(Memobase, Honcho), and OS-style managers (Letta, MemOS). The MCP tier (Basic Memory,
OpenMemory, claude-mem) cuts across all four with a local-first, client-agnostic angle.
